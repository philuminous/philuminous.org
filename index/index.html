Lineage of Energy Psychology

Homeopathy (1790s - Samuel Hahnemann)

The original vibrational medicine. Because experiments were conducted in vitro, placebo influence was excluded. Further independent research is required to clarify mechanisms and clinical significance.

Discover the Parts of You Ready to Return

• Emotional numbness or persistent emptiness
• Feeling incomplete or disconnected
• Chronic fatigue without clear explanation
• Difficulty moving forward in life
• Repeating emotional or relationship patterns
• Reduced sense of vitality or joy

Soul Retrieval work gently supports the reintegration of these withdrawn aspects so the individual may feel more present, grounded, and internally aligned.

The Shamanic Roots

The modern Western understanding of Soul Retrieval was significantly influenced by the work of Michael Harner, the American anthropologist and founder of the Foundation for Shamanic Studies. Through the development of Core Shamanism, Harner synthesized cross-cultural shamanic practices into a framework accessible to contemporary practitioners.

His work with shamanic journeying helped bridge traditional indigenous knowledge — particularly from South American and Amazonian lineages — into modern integrative healing environments. Subsequent teachers expanded these methods with greater therapeutic sensitivity while maintaining respect for their traditional origins.
